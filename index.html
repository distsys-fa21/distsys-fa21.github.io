CSE 40771/60771 Distributed Systems - Fall 2021
Prof. Douglas Thain
Department of Computer Science and Engineering
University of Notre Dame

A distributed system is any computer system consisting of multiple machines that work together on a common problem. Distributed systems appear in many areas of computing, including cloud computing, mobile computing, edge computing, the internet of things, aerospace systems, and more. Distributed systems have been both interesting and difficult to build because their components may be autonomous and highly failure-prone. Students will learn the fundamental principles of distributed systems, study examples of current distributed systems, and build their own distributed systems from scratch.

This will be a fun and challenging class for students who like to build working software systems. Distributed systems connects the very practical aspects of software engineering (e.g. like how to handle a network disconnection) and the fundamental principles of computers (e.g. whether a partitioned system can reach agreement.) The skills that you learn here will apply directly to advanced systems used in industry.
